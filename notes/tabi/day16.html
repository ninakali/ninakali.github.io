6th of May: Nara



Some of you probably can guess where we are today!


Welcome to Nara, the first "permanent" capital of Japan (from 710 to 784). It is a city with many Buddhist temples and free-roaming wild deer.


The big Buddhist temples here were founded around the same time as the capital itself. Many are designated as the national treasures or world heritage sites.


The deer are wild, some of them are polite and will bow to you if you bow to them, some are mean and will grab you by your clothes so you'd feed them.

They mostly camp around places that sell shika senbei ("deer cracker"), a treat one can give to a deer. It's very fun, the deer will chase you forever as long as you have a shika senbei in your hands.


Generally, deers cross the road with humans, following the traffic lights. But sometimes they just decide the grass looks great between the lanes.

There aren't that many photos today because we've spent most of the day at the Nara National Museum, and photos are prohibited inside of the museum. The museum is mostly Buddhist artifacts from 7th to 9th century, including multiple artifacts designated as "national treasures". The museum kind of expects the visitors to know a thing or two about Buddhism; otherwise it's just ancient statues and swords.
